# Dream Journal Specification

## Purpose

The Dream Journal helps users capture, explore, and reflect upon dreams in a structured and emotionally safe environment.

The goal is not dream prediction.

The goal is:
- dream recall
- personal reflection
- emotional awareness
- symbolic exploration
- pattern recognition

Dream interpretation remains user-led.

AI acts as a reflective companion.

---

# Core Philosophy

Reflection before interpretation.

Users should first engage with their own experience before receiving AI observations.

The system strengthens self-awareness rather than replacing intuition.

---

# User Goals

Users want to:

- remember dreams
- discover recurring themes
- track emotional patterns
- explore symbolism
- improve dream recall
- develop lucid dreaming practices
- maintain a long-term dream archive

---

# User Flow

## Dream Capture

User wakes up.

Opens Dream Journal.

Options:

- Quick Voice Capture
- Quick Text Capture
- Guided Capture

Goal:

Capture dream within 30 seconds.

---

## Guided Reflection

After saving:

System asks:

- What emotions were strongest?
- What felt most meaningful?
- Did any symbols stand out?
- Have you experienced this before?

User reflects first.

Only then is AI analysis available.

---

## AI Reflection

AI provides:

- emotional themes
- recurring patterns
- possible symbolic associations
- reflective questions

AI must avoid certainty.

Example:

Good:
"Water sometimes symbolizes emotion or change. Does that resonate with your experience?"

Bad:
"This dream means major life changes are coming."

---

## Lucid Dreaming Layer

Optional.

- dream signs

- lucid dream tracking

---

# AI Responsibilities

AI should:

- ask thoughtful questions
- identify patterns
- summarize emotions
- encourage reflection

AI should never:

- predict future events
- claim spiritual certainty
- encourage dependency
- diagnose conditions
